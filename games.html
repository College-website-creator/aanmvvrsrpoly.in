BADMINTON:

Badminton is a racket sport by either two opposing players(singles) or two opposing pairs(doubles).The badminton court is rectangular and is divided by a net.Players scores points by hitting a shuttlecock with their racket so that it passes over the net and lands in other side of the court









TABLE TENNIS:

table tennis is a racket sport derived from tennis but distinguished by it's plying surface being atop stationary table,rather than the court on which players stand.











KHOKHO:

Kho kho is played on a rectangular court with a central lane connecting two poles which are at either end of the court. During the game, nine players from the chasing team (attacking team) are on the field, with eight of them sitting (crouched) in the central lane, while three runners from the defending team run around the court and try to avoid being touched.









KABADDI:

Kabaddi is a contact team sport played between two teams of seven players, originating in ancient India.The objective of the game is for a single player on offense, referred to as a "raider", to run into the opposing team's half of the court, touch out as many of their players as possible, and return to their own half of the court, all without being tackled by the defenders in 30 seconds.









VOLLEYBALL:

Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players are separated by a net. Each team tries to score points by grounding a ball on the other team's court under organized rules.









SHOTPUT:

The shot put is a track and field event involving "putting" (throwing) a heavy spherical ball—the shot—as far as possible.











DISC THROW:

The discus throw also known as disc throw, is a track and field sport in which the participant athlete throws an oblate spheroid weight — called a discus — in an attempt to mark a farther distance than other competitors.









RUNNIG:

Running races are contests to determine which of the competitors is able to run a certain distance in the shortest time. Today, competitive running events make up the core of the sport of athletics.









CHESS:

Chess is a board game for two players. It is sometimes called international chess or Western chess to distinguish it from related games such as xiangqi (Chinese chess) and shogi (Japanese chess).
